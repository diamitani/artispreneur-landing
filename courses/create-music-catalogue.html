How to Create a Music Catalogue

Business 8 lessons

Build and organize your music catalogue for licensing opportunities and professional industry management.

Module 1

Why Every Artist Needs a Music Catalogue

8 min

Introduction to Music Catalogues

A well-organized music catalogue is essential for monetization and royalty collection. It makes it easier for music supervisors and sync agencies to find and license your work.

Organized catalogs increase the chances of sync placements in TV, film, and ads. They streamline the process of submitting your tracks for opportunities or sending them to collaborators.

Think of your catalogue as your professional portfolio — it's what industry professionals see when they're looking for music. Make it easy for them to find what they need.
Module 2

Categorizing Your Music

10 min

How to Categorize Your Music

Organize your tracks into categories that make them easy to find:

• Genres: Pop, Indie, Hip-Hop, R&B, Electronic, etc.
• Mood: Upbeat, Emotional, Dark, Energetic, Laid-back, etc.
• Instrumentation: Acoustic, Electronic, Full Band, Orchestral, etc.

Create a song database that includes title, description of mood and genre, and tags/keywords for easy searchability. This database is your primary tool for responding to sync licensing opportunities quickly.
Module 3

Digital Storage and Best Practices

9 min

Best Practices for Organizing Your Music

Store your files securely and make them easily shareable using cloud storage platforms like Google Drive, Dropbox, or iCloud.

Adopt clear naming conventions for each file: TrackName_Tempo_Genre_Version (e.g., "SongTitle_120BPM_Rock_V1"). This makes it easy to identify songs and their attributes at a glance.

Regularly back up your entire catalog to avoid data loss. Use redundant storage — local backup plus cloud backup. A lost catalog can set your career back years.
Module 4

Metadata and Tagging

10 min

Tagging and Metadata for Discoverability

Metadata is the information embedded in your audio file. It includes ISRC codes, songwriter and producer info, tempo, and other musical characteristics.

Proper metadata ensures:
• Proper payments from streaming services and licensing
• Discoverability for music supervisors searching for specific types of music

Essential metadata fields for each track:
• ISRC Code: Unique identifier for each recording
• Writer Info: Songwriters, composers, and publishers
• Contact Info: Who should be contacted for licensing

Incomplete or incorrect metadata means missed opportunities and lost revenue.
Module 5

Sharing Your Catalogue

9 min

Sharing Your Catalogue with Industry Professionals

Prepare tailored music libraries for different industry professionals. Custom playlists for specific sync opportunities make a much stronger impression than sending your entire catalog.

Platforms like Disco and SoundCloud Pro offer private sharing options for professional presentation. These platforms allow you to create curated playlists for specific opportunities and track who's listening.

When sharing, include context about each track — mood, instrumentation, and potential use cases. Make it easy for music supervisors to say yes.
Module 6

Maintaining Your Catalogue

7 min

Maintaining and Updating Your Catalogue

Keep your catalogue fresh by adding new releases and registering them with your P.R.O. Make sure metadata is accurate and up to date.

Ensure that all tracks have the latest information for contact info, ISRC codes, and songwriter details. A dated or inaccurate catalogue can cost you licensing opportunities.

Set a schedule — quarterly or bi-annually — to review and update your entire catalog. This maintenance is as important as creating new music.
Module 7

Protecting and Monetizing Your Catalogue

8 min

Frequently Asked Questions

Use watermarking or password-protected links when sharing sensitive files. Your catalogue is valuable intellectual property — treat it as such.

Monetize through sync licensing by submitting your tracks for use in TV, films, and commercials. Ensure your tracks are available on all major streaming platforms.

A well-maintained catalogue is an asset that generates revenue for years. The time you invest in organization pays dividends throughout your career.
Module 8

Keeping Your Catalogue Organized Long-Term

5 min

Course Conclusion

Organize your catalog by genre, mood, and instrumentation. Ensure metadata is complete for every track.

Regularly update your catalog, check metadata, and ensure your songs are registered with the proper rights organizations. A well-organized catalogue is a career asset that grows in value over time.

Start today — even organizing just five songs is progress toward a professional music catalogue.
