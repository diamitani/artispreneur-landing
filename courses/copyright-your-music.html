How to Copyright Your Music

Legal 7 lessons

Protect your creative works by learning how to properly copyright your music and understand your rights as a creator.

Module 1

What is a Copyright?

10 min

Introduction to Copyright

A copyright is a legal right granted to creators of original works, including music compositions and sound recordings. Key points:

• Copyright protection applies to literary, musical, artistic, and other works
• Once a work is copyrighted, the creator has the exclusive right to use, distribute, and license that work
• Music creators can copyright both the composition (musical notes, lyrics) and the sound recording (actual recorded performance)

Understanding copyright is the foundation of protecting your creative work and ensuring you get paid for its use.
Module 2

Types of Copyrights for Music

11 min

Types of Copyrights for Music

Composition Copyright protects the written music — melodies, lyrics, and other musical elements. This is often held by songwriters and composers.

Sound Recording Copyright protects the actual recorded performance of the composition. This is often held by the recording artist or record label.

Key distinction: The composition copyright covers the song itself (notes and lyrics), while the sound recording copyright covers the specific recorded version. Both are valuable and can be licensed separately.

Register both if you create and record your own music.
Module 3

How to Copyright Your Music

12 min

How to Copyright Your Music

Step-by-step guide to registering your music for copyright:

1. Visit the U.S. Copyright Office website at copyright.gov
2. Choose the correct form (Form SR for sound recordings, Form PA for compositions)
3. Complete the registration form with details about your work
4. Pay the filing fee (typically $35-$55 for a single work)
5. Submit your work, either digitally or physically

Decide whether to copyright a single song or a complete album based on your budget and release strategy. Each individual work should be registered to fully protect your catalog.
Module 4

Benefits of Copyright Protection

9 min

Benefits of Copyrighting Your Work

Copyright ownership gives you the legal right to decide how your music is used. It prevents unauthorized use and ensures you control all commercial and non-commercial applications.

Monetize your music through licensing: TV, film, advertising, and games. Your copyright becomes an intellectual property asset that can appreciate over time.

Once registered, your copyright serves as a legal shield against infringement. You can defend your work in court if someone uses it without permission. Registration is required before you can file a lawsuit.
Module 5

Enforcing Your Copyright

10 min

Enforcing Your Copyright

Monitor your music for unauthorized use. If you discover infringement, assert your rights and demand compensation.

The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) allows you to request removal of infringing content from platforms like YouTube, Facebook, and streaming services. File a DMCA takedown notice with the platform hosting the infringing content.

If someone infringes and refuses to comply, you can pursue legal action. Understand statutory damages and seek legal representation when necessary.
Module 6

International Copyright Protection

8 min

Frequently Asked Questions

Copyright laws work internationally through treaties and agreements. Your U.S. copyright is recognized in many countries through international conventions.

Common misconceptions about copyright:
• Automatic copyright exists the moment you create a work, but formal registration provides additional legal benefits
• Registering is the only way to sue for infringement
• Copyright covers both the composition AND the sound recording

Expected costs range from $35-$55 per work, with processing taking 1-3 months in most cases.
Module 7

After You Register

6 min

Course Conclusion and Next Steps

After registering your copyrights, monitor your registered works and use your copyrights to explore licensing opportunities.

Key takeaways:
• Register every original work you create
• Understand the difference between composition and sound recording copyrights
• Use your copyrights to generate income through licensing

Taking these steps ensures your creative legacy is protected and monetized properly.
