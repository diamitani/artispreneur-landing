Learn how to distribute your music to all major streaming platforms and digital stores worldwide.

Module 1

What is a DSP?

9 min

Introduction to Digital Distribution

A Digital Service Provider (DSP) is an online platform that distributes music to streaming services, allowing your songs to be available on Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon Music, and other platforms.

Key points:
• DSPs handle the logistics of getting your music on multiple streaming services
• You retain ownership of your music while DSPs distribute it
• Using a DSP ensures your music reaches a global audience

Revenue is generated every time your music is streamed or downloaded. DSPs handle the backend work, freeing you to focus on creating.
Module 2

Comparing Distribution Platforms

12 min

Comparing Popular Distribution Platforms

Each DSP has different strengths. Here's a comparison of major platforms:

• TuneCore: Charges per release. Offers distribution to over 150 stores plus publishing administration and sync licensing.
• CD Baby: One-time fee with global distribution, physical distribution for CDs and vinyl, and YouTube monetization.
• DistroKid: Unlimited releases for a flat annual fee. Fast distribution with customizable splits.
• Amuse: Free tier available with premium upgrade options.
• UnitedMasters: Focuses on artist independence with brand deal opportunities.

Choose based on your release frequency, budget, and need for additional services.
Module 3

How to Upload and Distribute

11 min

How to Distribute Your Music

Step-by-step guide to uploading your music:

1. Sign up with your preferred DSP
2. Upload your tracks — ensure they're properly mixed, mastered, and in the correct format (WAV or FLAC)
3. Fill in metadata: track names, artist names, genres, and release date
4. Schedule your release for maximum impact

Before uploading, make sure your music is ready:
• High-quality audio in WAV or FLAC
• Album artwork at least 3000x3000 pixels
• ISRC and UPC codes collected (some DSPs generate these for you)
Module 4

Maximizing Revenue from DSPs

10 min

Maximizing Revenue from DSPs

Royalties from DSPs include mechanical royalties from streams and downloads, public performance royalties from radio and public spaces, and publishing royalties for songwriters.

DistroKid and similar platforms allow you to automatically split royalties with collaborators. Set up individual royalty percentages to ensure everyone gets paid fairly.

Use analytics from DSP platforms to track listener locations and demographics, identify which platforms and playlists drive the most streams, and make data-driven decisions about your release strategy.
Module 5

Choosing the Right DSP for You

9 min

Choosing the Best DSP for Your Needs

Compare pricing models: subscription vs. per-release. DistroKid charges an annual fee for unlimited releases, while TuneCore charges per release.

Additional services to consider:
• Sync licensing opportunities to get your music in TV and film
• Publishing administration to collect royalties globally
• Customer support availability and quality

The best DSP for you depends on your specific needs, budget, and career stage. You can always switch later, though it requires taking down and re-uploading your catalog.
Module 6

Distribution FAQ

8 min

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you switch DSPs after releasing a song? Yes, but it requires removing your music from one DSP and re-uploading to another, which can disrupt streams and playlist placements.

How much do DSPs take in fees? Each DSP takes a small percentage as a fee. Some charge per release, others through subscriptions.

Getting on editorial playlists requires using submission tools provided by streaming platforms (like Spotify for Artists) and networking with playlist curators.

Start with one DSP that matches your needs and release consistently.
