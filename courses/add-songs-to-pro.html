How to Add Songs to Your PRO

Royalties 7 lessons

Step-by-step guide to registering your songs with your Performance Rights Organization to collect royalties.

Module 1

What is a P.R.O.?

9 min

Introduction to Performance Rights Organizations

A Performing Rights Organization (P.R.O.) collects public performance royalties on behalf of songwriters and publishers when their music is played on the radio, TV, live venues, or streaming platforms.

The three major P.R.O.s in the U.S. are:
• ASCAP: Non-profit organization, widely used by independent artists
• BMI: Similar to ASCAP, free to join for songwriters
• SESAC: Invitation-only, offers more personalized service

Registering with a P.R.O. ensures you get paid whenever your music is publicly performed — whether on the radio, at a venue, or streaming online.
Module 2

How to Sign Up with a P.R.O.

10 min

How to Sign Up with a P.R.O.

Choosing the right P.R.O. depends on your needs. Compare membership fees, ease of use, reporting systems, and additional benefits like songwriter events or tools.

To sign up:
1. Visit the P.R.O. website (ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC)
2. Create an account with your personal and music-related information
3. Agree to the membership terms

ASCAP and BMI are free for songwriters with a small fee for publishers. SESAC is invitation-only with no public fee structure. Choose the one that best aligns with your career goals.
Module 3

Registering Your Songs

11 min

How to Add Songs to Your P.R.O. Account

Once registered as a member, you can add compositions to your account. Log in and add a new composition with the title, genre, and details about the work.

Critical steps:
• Include co-writers and add their splits, making sure percentages add up to 100%
• Add each co-writer's P.R.O. ID
• Include publisher information if applicable
• Double-check song titles and spellings

Registering a song does not transfer ownership — it ensures royalties are collected and distributed according to the splits you set.
Module 4

Ensuring Proper Registration

8 min

Claiming Tracks and Ensuring Proper Registration

If you discover that someone else has registered your song, check the registration to see if it's correct and claim your portion if listed as a co-writer.

P.R.O.s allow you to submit setlists and claim live performances. Submit performance details for concerts or tours, and claim radio airplay if your music was played on broadcast or online radio.

Common registration errors to avoid:
• Misspelled song titles
• Incorrect splits
• Leaving out co-writers
Module 5

Collecting Your Royalties

9 min

Collecting Royalties from Your P.R.O.

P.R.O.s pay public performance royalties including radio and TV airplay, live performances, and streaming royalties from platforms like Spotify and YouTube.

To ensure you're collecting everything you're owed:
• Check quarterly statements for accuracy
• Submit live performance reports to claim royalties from concerts
• If your music is played internationally, P.R.O.s collect through reciprocal agreements

Consider signing with a sub-publisher if you have significant overseas activity to maximize international royalty collection.
Module 6

PRO Best Practices and FAQ

7 min

Frequently Asked Questions

Typically, it takes 1-2 weeks for a song to be processed and appear in your P.R.O. account. If you don't register your songs, you won't collect performance royalties even if your music is played on the radio.

You can update registration details by logging into your account and editing the song's information. Contact your P.R.O. if you need help correcting an error.

Key takeaway: register every song, double-check everything, and stay on top of your quarterly statements.
Module 7

Keeping Your PRO Catalog Updated

5 min

Course Conclusion

Register your music with your P.R.O. as soon as it's released. Check for errors in song titles, splits, and co-writer details.

Regularly update your P.R.O. account with new songs and correct any errors in older registrations to maximize your royalty collection. This is an ongoing process — make it part of your release routine.
