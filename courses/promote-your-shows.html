How to Promote Your Own Shows

Live Performance 7 lessons

Discover proven strategies for promoting your live shows, increasing attendance, and building a loyal fanbase.

Module 1

Why Book Your Own Shows?

8 min

Introduction to Self-Booking

Booking your own shows gives you control over your performance schedule, the ability to choose venues that align with your audience, and valuable experience in managing your career directly.

Self-promotion helps you build your brand and audience, gain valuable marketing experience, and increase your visibility. Every show you book and promote is a learning opportunity that builds skills you'll use throughout your career.

Start small and build momentum. Your first self-booked show doesn't need to be at a major venue — it just needs to happen.
Module 2

Finding and Contacting Venues

10 min

Finding and Contacting Venues

When researching venues, consider the size and type of venue that fits your music style. Check out other artists' shows to see what venues might be a good fit for your sound.

When reaching out to venue owners and bookers:
• Send a professional email with your press kit
• Be clear about your availability, expected draw, and type of performance
• Follow up if you don't hear back within a week

Build relationships with venue staff. A good relationship can lead to repeat bookings and better terms.
Module 3

Preparing for Your Show

11 min

Preparing for Your Show

A professional press kit should include a bio, high-quality photos, links to your music and videos, and contact information. This is your professional introduction.

When budgeting for a show:
• Include costs for venue rental, promotion, travel, and equipment
• Estimate potential income from ticket sales and merchandise
• Plan for contingencies

Promote your show using social media ads, creating event pages, posting flyers, and reaching out to local press. Start promotion at least 3-4 weeks before the show date.
Module 4

Show Day Execution

9 min

Day of the Show

On show day, arrive early for soundcheck. Make sure all equipment is working properly and coordinate with the sound engineer. A good soundcheck sets the tone for the entire performance.

During your show, interact with the audience to create a memorable experience. Encourage fans to follow you on social media and join your mailing list. The connection you build during a live show is more powerful than any online interaction.

Have merch available and a way to capture email addresses or followings.
Module 5

After the Show

8 min

After the Show

After your performance, thank the venue owners and staff. Ask for feedback and discuss future opportunities. A thank-you note goes a long way in building lasting relationships.

Gather fan feedback by asking for comments on social media and encouraging fans to share photos and videos from the show. User-generated content is powerful promotion for your next event.

Review your performance by watching video recordings and reflecting on what went well and what could be improved. Each show is a chance to get better.
Module 6

Overcoming Common Challenges

7 min

Frequently Asked Questions

If you can't afford to rent a venue, consider alternatives like house concerts, community centers, or partnering with other artists to share costs.

If you experience low ticket sales, promote harder in the days leading up to the event, offer discounts or bundle deals with merchandise, and focus on delivering a great performance to those who attend.

Every show is a building block. Small audiences become bigger audiences over time as you build your reputation and following.
Module 7

Building Your Live Career

6 min

Course Conclusion and Next Steps

Booking and promoting your own shows helps you gain control and experience. Preparation and promotion are key to success.

Continue booking shows, expanding to new venues, and building a loyal fanbase through consistent performances. As you grow, consider working with booking agents and promoters to scale your live career.

The skills you learn from self-booking — negotiation, promotion, logistics — are invaluable throughout your entire music career.
